Impact explainer


Impact Thesis

Ecotrust Forests II is designed to have specific conservation and social impacts that are strong drivers of the Funds competitive advantage.
The Manager plans to identify and acquire high priority, ecologically significant forestland in the region, with the goal of demonstrating a sustainable forest management strategy and ultimately transitioning ownership to local land stewards like land trusts, state, federal and local agencies, conservation groups or tribes.
During the term of the Fund, the Manager plans to actively engage in ecosystem-based forestry and manage the forests towards long term sustainability, improving forest health and enhanced carbon sequestration.
The Manager also plans on securing ecologically important features of a property via conservation easements or permanent legal obligations that would ensure the conservation uplift created via its Management.

A closer look to the impact

A closer look to the impact of sustainable forest management on their long-term sustainability, carbon sequestration capacity and rural job creation.
